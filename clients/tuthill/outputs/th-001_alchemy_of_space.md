# Design Philosophy Post: The Alchemy of Space

## Instagram Content — Tuthill Design

---

## Image Direction

**Required:** Interior photograph demonstrating dramatic light and shadow

**Ideal shot:**
- Strong natural light from single source (window, skylight)
- Deep shadows creating architectural contrast
- Visible texture (plaster, wood grain, linen, stone)
- Minimal staging — space as subject
- High contrast, slightly desaturated edit

**Fallback:** Any portfolio image with strong light/shadow interplay

---

## Caption

```
The alchemy of space.

It begins before the furniture. Before the palette. Before the first swatch is pulled.

It begins with light.

Where does it enter? How does it fall? What does it reveal—and what does it choose to conceal?

A room without intention is just a container. Four walls holding air.

But a room that understands its light—that breathes with it, shifts with it, responds to the angle of the sun as the day moves—that room becomes something else entirely.

It becomes felt.

This is what we chase. Not trends. Not moments. Not the thing that photographs well but lives poorly.

We chase the space that changes how you stand in it. The room that makes you pause at the threshold. The light that makes you look up.

Alchemy. Intention. Resonance.

That's the work.

—

```
